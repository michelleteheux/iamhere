7 Top Benefits of Being an ER Travel Nurse

Discover why emergency room travel nursing offers unique advantages for your career and lifestyle.

Explore Benefits

Why Choose ER Travel Nursing?

Emergency Room travel nursing combines the fast-paced excitement of emergency medicine with the freedom and flexibility of travel healthcare. It's a career path that offers both professional growth and personal adventure.

As an ER travel nurse, you'll work in various healthcare settings across the country, bringing your critical care skills to facilities that need them most while enjoying numerous benefits that staff positions can't match.

7 Top Benefits of ER Travel Nursing

Discover the advantages that make ER travel nursing an attractive career choice

1

Higher Earning Potential

ER travel nurses typically earn 15-25% more than their staff counterparts, with additional bonuses, overtime opportunities, and completion incentives.

Many assignments include housing stipends, travel reimbursements, and license reimbursement, further increasing your take-home pay.

Average ER travel nurse salary: $90,000 - $130,000 annually
2

Professional Diversity & Skill Development

Working in different ER settings exposes you to varied patient populations, protocols, and technologies, accelerating your clinical expertise.

From Level I trauma centers to rural critical access hospitals, each assignment builds unique skills that enhance your resume and marketability.

Gain experience with diverse EHR systems and emergency protocols
3

Flexibility & Control Over Your Schedule

Choose assignments that fit your lifestyle - work during peak seasons and take extended time off between contracts to travel or pursue personal interests.

With typical contracts lasting 13 weeks, you have the freedom to explore different regions without long-term commitment.

Take 4-8 weeks off between assignments while maintaining benefits
4

Travel & Adventure Opportunities

Experience living in different parts of the country while getting paid. From coastal cities to mountain towns, each assignment is a new adventure.

Many agencies offer travel reimbursement and housing assistance, making relocation seamless and affordable.

Explore new regions while building your career
5

Comprehensive Benefits Packages

Most travel nursing agencies offer health insurance, dental and vision coverage, retirement plans with matching, and professional liability insurance.

These benefits often rival or exceed those offered by traditional staff positions, providing security while you travel.

Health insurance typically begins on your first day of assignment
6

Minimal Workplace Politics

As a travel nurse, you can focus on patient care without getting caught up in long-term departmental politics or institutional bureaucracy.

Your temporary status often allows you to maintain professional boundaries while still forming meaningful connections with colleagues.

Focus on clinical excellence rather than office dynamics
7

Career Advancement Opportunities

Travel nursing experience is highly valued by employers and can lead to leadership roles, specialty certifications, and higher-paying positions.

Many travel nurses use their diverse experience to transition into roles such as nurse educators, clinical coordinators, or healthcare consultants.

Build a standout resume with diverse clinical experiences
